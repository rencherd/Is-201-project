Detailed Restoration Steps

Here are the detailed steps involved in restoring my classic F100 truck.

Home

1. How I Got It

I bought the Truck in Mountain green Utah, it barely ran for a few minutes before stalling, and all 4 corners were rusted out.

2. Disassembly

In this phase, I removed the major components of the truck to inspect the frame, engine, and body for any necessary repairs.

3. Welding and Fabrication

Here, I performed necessary welding and fabricated replacement parts for the truck’s frame and body.

4. Paint Removal

In this step, I stripped off the old paint and rust to prepare for the new powder coat and primer.

5. Powder Coating

After prepping the metal, I applied a durable powder coat to the frame and other parts to protect against rust and improve appearance.

6. Epoxy Primer

To the Body, I applied an epoxy primer to the body parts, providing a solid base for the topcoat paint.

7. Bodywork

Bodywork was done to smooth out imperfections and ensure the truck’s body is in perfect shape for painting.

8. Final Primer

After bodywork, a final layer of primer was applied to ensure a smooth and even surface before painting.

9. Painting

The truck was now ready for its new paint job, which is an important step in restoring its original look. Most of this process was done by a shop while I was on my mission.

10. After Paint

Once the paint was applied, I moved on to final detailing to bring out the shine and smoothness of the new finish.

11. Reassembly

After everything was painted and primed, I began reassembling the truck, putting all the parts back together to make it whole again.
